What you need to get started blacksmithing

This is a short list about the stuff you would need to get into blacksmithing

To start, your going to need an anvil and forge

First of all, you'll need a forge and an anvil. For the forge, there are bacically two options, you could make the forge yourself, or you could just buy one. Forges come in many diffrent shapes, sizes, and fuel systems, but for startig out it would be in your best intrest to buy a gas forge as they are much less work that one that runs on coal or coke. As far as the anvil goes, you genrally want an anvil that has a relativley flat working surface, meaning that there aren't a bunch of divets from where pervious owners might have bee working, it's fine if there are divets on the shelf between the horn and the working surface. When buying an anvil used, you should tap o the working surface and listen for dead sounding spots, indicating the steel surface is delaminating from the cast iron. Also keep in mind that it's a good idea to check for welds because this could mean that the anvil has been resurface at home and could possibly delaminate depending on what type of metal they were using for the welding

You will additionally need some safety gear and tools

You'll need some tools to go with that avil and forge. Lets start with some basic safety gear. You should always have a thick leather apron to protect your clothes from sparks and other fire hazardes, you should have a good range of hammers of diffrent weights for diffrent jobs, you should always have saftey glasses on, always wear closed toe shoes or boots, get a thick pair of welding or just heavy leather gloves, get a good size, heavy vice, and get some tongs.

That is all you really need to get started with blacksmithing.
